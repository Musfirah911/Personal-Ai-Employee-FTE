We've been building a local-first AI Employee for the past two weeks.

Not a chatbot. Not a co-pilot. An autonomous agent that reads your inbox, drafts replies, schedules content, and logs every action — all without touching a single cloud server.

Here are 3 things we didn't expect to learn:

**1. The hardest problem isn't intelligence. It's trust.**

The agent can draft a perfect email in seconds. But knowing *when* to send it — and when to pause and ask — is an entirely different problem. We built a `/Pending_Approval` layer for every action that touches the outside world. Human-in-the-loop isn't a limitation. It's the product.

**2. Local-first forces better architecture.**

When your data never leaves your machine, you stop relying on SaaS magic and start thinking in files, watchers, and event loops. The system is simpler, faster, and fully auditable. Every action is a Markdown file. Every log is a JSON line.

**3. The "employee" framing changes everything.**

We stopped asking "what can AI automate?" and started asking "what would a great employee do here?" That shift produced a system with a Dashboard, a Company Handbook, a skills library, and a clear chain of command. It behaves like a junior analyst, not a black box.

Two weeks in. Bronze → Silver tier complete.

The Digital FTE is real — and it costs less than a coffee subscription per hour of work.

What are you automating in your business right now? 👇
