Let's do the math that nobody talks about.

**A human FTE:**
- Cost: $4,000–$8,000/month (salary + overhead)
- Available hours: ~2,000/year (40 hrs/week, minus PTO, sick days, meetings)
- Effective cost per hour: $24–$48

**A Digital FTE:**
- Cost: $500–$2,000/month (AI subscriptions + compute)
- Available hours: 8,760/year (24/7/365, no PTO)
- Effective cost per hour: $0.06–$0.23

The math is undeniable. A Digital FTE is 100–200x cheaper per hour of availability.

**But here's the nuance most people miss:**

A Digital FTE doesn't replace a human. It *amplifies* one.

The highest-value use isn't replacing a junior employee. It's eliminating the 40% of a senior employee's week spent on:
- Inbox triage
- Status updates
- Routine reporting
- Scheduling and follow-ups
- Drafting first versions of everything

When you give a $10k/month senior team member a Digital FTE, you don't save $4k — you unlock $4k of compounding leverage. They operate at their ceiling, not their floor.

That's the real ROI.

Not replacement. Amplification.

The companies that figure this out in 2026 will have a workforce productivity advantage that compounds year over year.

Are you building a Digital FTE layer into your team yet?
