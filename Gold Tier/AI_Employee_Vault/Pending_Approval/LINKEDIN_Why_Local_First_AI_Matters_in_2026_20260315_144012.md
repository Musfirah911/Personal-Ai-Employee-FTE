Every AI tool in 2026 wants the same thing: your data in their cloud.

Your emails. Your files. Your client conversations. Your business logic. All uploaded, indexed, and retained on servers you don't control — in exchange for a monthly subscription and a privacy policy nobody reads.

We said no.

Our AI Employee runs 100% locally. No data leaves the machine. No API calls to process your inbox. No third-party servers storing your business context.

Here's what that decision actually changed:

**Privacy became a default, not a feature.**
Client emails stay on your machine. Drafts are Markdown files in a local vault. The agent's memory is your file system — auditable, portable, and owned by you.

**The system became transparent.**
When AI lives in the cloud, it's a black box. When it runs locally, it's a file watcher, a Python loop, and a skills library. You can read every line. You can stop it instantly. You understand exactly what it does and why.

**Reliability stopped depending on uptime.**
No API rate limits. No service outages. No "we're experiencing higher than normal volume" messages. The agent runs when you run it, on your hardware, at your pace.

The tradeoff? You do the setup once. You own the infrastructure.

But in 2026, when data is leverage — local-first isn't just a preference.

It's a competitive moat.
